The Woman Reinventing Plastic: Catia Bastioli and Novamont

Who is she?

Catia Bastioli is an Italian researcher, chemist and entrepreneur, who has always been fond of chemistry.

Her career started as a researcher in Montedison, the largest research group in Italy, and this is where she used her chemistry expertise to develop her bioplastic. Here she helped found a research centre that then became Novamont.[1a]

Novamont

Global warming is a very important and current problem. However there are some ways to help with it. Novamont have taken it upon themselves to contribute to the help.

Their goal is to manage solid waste disposal with regard to the decreasing availability of landfills, the litter problem and the pollution of the marine environment. This is an issue as all of this waste is becoming a very urgent issue in industrialized countries and it may extend very quickly to developing countries.

A solution they have come up with is the development of MATER-BI, which is a range of bioplastics.

These bioplastics are created with technologies which use starches, cellulose, vegetable oils, and their combinations.[1b]

These MATER-BI bioplastics look identical to regular plastic and they behave very similarly, but what makes the difference is that MATER-BI is biodegradable and compostable.
