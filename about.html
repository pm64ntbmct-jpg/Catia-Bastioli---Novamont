About Catia Bastioli

Get to Know Catia Bastioli

Bastioli's persistent love for chemistry lead her to the University of Perugia where she graduated in pure chemistry. She then went on to attend the Business Management School at Bocconi University, before starting her career in materials science and environmental sustainability at Montedison's Corporate Research Centre.

This is where she realised she could apply her knowledge to create bioplastics. Alongside this, she also aided in the founding of a research centre here that became Novamont.[2a]

One of her aims with bioplastics has been to reduce environmental impact and you can see this with what her Novamont company does.

Achievements and Awards

European Inventor of the Year

In 2007, Bastioli won the European Inventor of the Year award in the category "SMEs/research". This was because of her revolutional technology of bioplastic. At this point in time, Novamont had their bio-degradable waste bags in use in over 3,500 cities.[2b]

Person of the Year

In 2017, Bastioli won the Bioplastic - Person of the Year award. This was also due to her revolutionising the bioplastic industry. She took charge and paved the way for bioplastics and has changed the world for the better since and will continue to do so. [2c]

Maria Bakunin Medal

In 2025, Bastioli was awarded the Maria Bakunin Medal by the Italian Chemical Society. The award was given to her as recognition for her "pionerring commitment to the industrial development of innovative and sustainable materials." [2d]
